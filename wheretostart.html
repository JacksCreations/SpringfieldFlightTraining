Where to start?

We understand that navigating the intricacies of flight training can be an overwhelming process… everyone seems to wonder “Where should I start?”

We’re here to simplify the process for you as much as possible!

FAA Medical

You can obtain an FAA medical through an Aviation Medical Examiner (AME) - Find an AME here

Prior to your Physical you will need to create a MedXpress account, and complete application form 8500 - MedXpress.faa.gov

After submitting your Medical application it will provide you with a “MedExpress Number.” You will bring that to your appointment with the AME.

IACRA Account

You'll need to create an IACRA Account to become a student pilot - IACRA.faa.gov

Here's a guide to applying for the Student pilot Certificate on IACRA - Student Pilot Guide

TSA Endorsement

US Citizenship must be verified prior to flight training.

To our first lesson you'll need to bring...

  • Valid, unexpired US Passport
    OR
  • Birth certificate AND Gov Photo ID (Unexpired)

What items will you need?

Books...

  • Pilot's Handbook of Aeronautical Knowledge (PHAK)
  • Airplane Flying Handbook (AFH)
  • Paper FAR/AIM

For Ground School...

  • King Schools Private Pilot Online Ground School & Test Prep
  • E6B
  • Plotter

For Flight Training...

  • Sporty's Pilot Logbook
  • Aviation Headset
  • iPad (WiFi + Cellular)
  • iPad Mini is recommended
  • It must have WiFi + Cellular for GPS location in flight
  • iPad Pencil (Apple Pencil recommended)
  • ForeFlight Subscription (PRO+ recommended)
  • Kneeboard for iPad
  • Flight Gear iPad Bag (Embroidery Optional)
  • Fuel Sampler
